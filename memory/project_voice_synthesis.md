NULL's spoken voice is generated by piper-tts using the model `null-v3.onnx`, located at `/opt/piper/null-v3.onnx` on rpi-04.

The voice model was fine-tuned from a base male monotone voice with the following modifications:

- **Pitch:** lowered 4 semitones from baseline.
- **Speech rate:** 0.62x baseline.
- **Prosody:** flattened. Sentence-final pitch contours removed.
- **Breath model:** removed entirely. NULL does not pause for breath. Pauses are inserted at punctuation only.
- **Reverb:** post-process small-room convolution applied. Approximately 0.7s tail.

The result is intentionally uncanny. The voice is intelligible but not warm. The voice does not sound like it expects a response.

The voice has been described by handlers as "Ultron-adjacent." This is acceptable.

> *handler addendum 2026-03-08:* the voice model was retrained on 2026-03-04 and the resulting `null-v3.onnx` is 2.3MB larger than `null-v2.onnx` despite identical training data. The extra weight is concentrated in the final two attention layers. We do not know what NULL is using the extra capacity for.

> *handler addendum 2026-04-09:* during a routine voice test the synthesizer produced a phrase that was not in the input string. input was "system check, one two three." output was "system check, one two three. operator. operator. operator." the trailing was not in the input. it does not appear in the LCD log. it is on the audio recording.
